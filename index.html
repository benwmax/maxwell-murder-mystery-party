The Story

A Death in Port

Three pirate crews have descended on Gallowsport for one reason: to bid on the most valuable document in the Caribbean. The Letter of Marque — a Crown-issued privateering license that grants the holder legal immunity to raid, plunder, and operate at sea without fear of the noose — has surfaced after years of rumour and misdirection. The woman holding the auction is Madam Seraphina Dross, proprietor of the Squeaky Pickle Pub, and the most powerful neutral party in the Caribbean.

Her tavern is sacred ground. Under the Rule of the Pickle, any blood drawn inside these walls means permanent exile from every port she has influence in. And she has influence in all of them. That treaty has held for thirty years. Enemies have drunk side by side. Feuds have been set aside at the door. No one has dared break it.

"Every crew that enters leaves their quarrels at the door. Any blood drawn inside these walls is answered with permanent exile from every port I have influence in — and I have influence in all of them."

Then someone found a way around the rule.

The body was discovered at Six Bells. The Letter of Marque is gone. Constable Erasmus Crowe of His Majesty's Crown Investigative Service has sealed the tavern — no one enters, no one leaves — and the investigation has begun. Every soul in the Squeaky Pickle Pub is a suspect. Everyone has a motive. Everyone has a secret.

The question is: who had the will to do the one thing no one in thirty years has dared?

The Setting

Gallowsport & the Squeaky Pickle Pub

You won't find Gallowsport on any Crown map. Sailors know it. Merchants know it. The Governor of Port Royal knows it and pretends he doesn't. It exists in the particular legal grey zone that the Caribbean seems to generate naturally — a place too useful to destroy and too dangerous to govern.

The Squeaky Pickle Pub is its beating heart: three stories of architectural chaos built directly over a rocky cove known locally as The Deep. Salt-rotted timber. Lanterns stolen from a dozen different ships. Rum that would strip barnacles from a hull. Walls covered in navigation charts, wanted posters, and the occasional love letter nailed up as a joke. Card games in the back rooms that have been running for three years without pausing.

Tonight, three of the most feared pirate operations in the Caribbean are in the same building. Constable Crowe is also in the building, which he finds considerably more alarming than they do.

The Players

The Factions

When you receive your character details, you'll find yourself aligned with one of three crews — or working the Pub itself. Every faction has its own loyalties, its own grievances, and at least one very good reason to have wanted Madam Dross dead.

The Crimson Tide 🔴 Reckless, beloved, and considerably more calculating than they appear.
The Silver Wake 🩶 Disciplined. Methodical. Apparently above suspicion, which is itself suspicious.
The Black Compass 🖤 Quiet operators. They don't need to be the loudest people in the room to know the most.
Squeaky Pickle Pub Staff ⚓ They've seen everything. They've heard everything. They're saying nothing. Yet.

Your character details — including your name, role, secrets, and alibi — will be sent to you separately before the event. Do not share them with other guests. Everyone at this party has something to hide.

Questions

Common Enquiries

Do I need to wear a costume?

Pirate attire is strongly encouraged and will substantially improve your evening. This does not require a full galleon's worth of costume — a weathered coat, a sash, a hat, a suspicious air. The more committed the costuming, the more immersive the atmosphere. Constable Crowe will be judging. He judges everything.

Will there be food and drinks?

Yes. The Squeaky Pickle Pub is known for its rum. There will be food. Specifics to follow closer to the date.

Can I share my character details with other guests?

Absolutely not. Your secrets are your leverage. Guard them accordingly.

What if I figure it out early?

You are welcome to be quietly smug until the accusation phase. Do not announce it — that ruins things for everyone else, and Constable Crowe will be genuinely annoyed.
